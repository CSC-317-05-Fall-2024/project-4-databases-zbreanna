Water Dog Lake

Waterdog Lake is more of a reservoir than a lake as it is defined by a big dam across the valley. Nowadays they keep the water level low and the water is apparently used for feeding the stream below. There is a dock that provides access to the big drain underneath the lake. The lake is always teaming with wildlife.

Twin Pines Park

Twin Pines Park is a popular recreational area located in Belmont. It spans approximately 10 acres and is known for its scenic beauty, featuring lush greenery, mature trees, and a tranquil creek running through the park. It hosts several community events with the historic Manor House and Twin Pines Art Center. The park is a beloved spot for families, nature enthusiasts, and those seeking a peaceful outdoor experience.

Crystal Springs Regional Trail

The Crystal Springs Regional Trail is a scenic and popular trail system. It spans approximately 17.5 miles and is divided into three segments: the San Andreas, Crystal Springs, and Sawyer Camp Trails. The trail follows the path of the San Andreas Fault and offers stunning views of the Crystal Springs Reservoir, oak woodlands, and rolling hills. visitors can enjoy the beautiful natural scenery of the San Francisco Peninsula.
